Scale

Weigh all of the ingredients before you begin. In this case, the milk and eggs can be cold from the refigerator, but the butter needs to be very soft (not melted) so that it can be incorporated into the dough by hand.

  • 156 grams whole milk
  • 256 grams whole eggs
  • 72 grams sugar
  • 6 grams yeast
  • 180 grams very soft butter
  • 600 grams all-purpose flour
  • 12 grams salt
  • Kitchen scale

Combine

Combine the milk, eggs, sugar, yeast, and butter in a large bowl using a whisk or a fork.

  • 156 grams whole milk
  • 256 grams whole eggs
  • 72 grams sugar
  • 6 grams yeast
  • 180 grams very soft butter
  • Large bowl
  • Whisk or fork

Mix

Add the flour and salt on top of the wet ingredients and mix by hand, feeling that the ingredients are well combined, until a smooth, even, wet dough is produced. Don't worry if it seems very relaxed. The soft butter will firm up with the wolder ingredients in the dough.

Rest

Cover the dough with a floured kitchen towel, and let it rest for 20 to 30 minutes while the flour absorbs the liquid ingredients.

  • Flour for kitchen towel
  • Kitchen towel

Knead

Flour your work surface and turn the dough out onto it. Knead the dough by pushing forward into the dough with the heels of your hands and then folding the elongated dough back toward you. Give it a quarter turn, push the dough away, and fold it back again. Keep with it until you can feel the dough has gotten tightened up and gotten smoother, 5 to 10 minutes.

  • Flour for work surface

Rise

Place the dough in a large bowl and cover with a floured kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let rise for 1 hour 30 minutes to 2 hours.

  • Flour if using kitchen towel
  • Large bowl
  • Kitchen towel or plastic wrap

Make the Filling

In a medium bowl, combine the butter, suga, cinnamon, and vanilla with a spatula and set aside.

  • 110 grams soft butter
  • 250 grams brown sugar
  • 15 grams cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • Medium bowl
  • Spatula

Roll Out

Flour your work surface. Take the dough form the refrigerator, if chilled, and roll it out with a rolling pin to a rectangle about 24 inches by 8 inches. The longer side should be horizontal.

  • Flour for work surface
  • Rolling pin

Shape

Using a rubber spatula, spread about 2/4 of the filling over the dough, covering the surface but leaving a small strip of dough uncovered on the long side farthest from you; you will use this to seal the edge of the dough. Gently roll up the dough, pushing the edge of the dough away from you and rolling it up like a yoga mat. Seal the seam by pressing the dough together. Using a metal dough scrapper, cut the spiral into 12 (2-inch) rolls.
If your roasting pan doesn't have a nonstick surface, line it with parchment paper or aluminum foil. Spread the remaining cinnamon-sugar filling over the bottom of the pan, then place the rolls into the pan cut-side up, spacing them evenly apart so that they have room to rise.

  • Rubber spatula
  • Metal dough scrapper
  • 9-by-13-inch nonstick roasting pan
  • Optional: parchment or aluminum foil

Proof

At this point, you have two options: You can proof and bake the rolls, or you can refrigerate them from 2 hours to overnight and then proof and bake them. Whichever option you choose, proof for 1 hour 30 minutes to 2 hours, until they are puffy and full of air and feel like marshmallow. When pressed with a finger, it should leave an indent instead of sprining back up.

Preheat

While the dough is a proofing, preheat your oven to 375°F.

Bake

Bake for 25 to 40 minutes, until browned on top. Take a sheet pan or large platter, place it over the roasting pan, and invert to release the cinnamon rolls.

  • Sheet pan or large platter

Cool

Let cool for 30 minutes before serving. I like to top them with flaky sea salt, but this is optional.

  • Optional: Flaky sea salt
